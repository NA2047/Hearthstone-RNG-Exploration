Has there been an increase in the Random component of cards within the game of Hearthstone ?

Where is all the RANDOM

Results:

After examining the results, I was expecting the chart to show a clear increase in the RNG component of Hearthstone. However, the table does not necessarily indicate this. What we can see is that an expansion like Un’Goro had a more substantial increase than any previous expansion, both in the percentage of the total set as well as by the number of cards. The introduction of the Adapt mechanic can explain a portion of this. Adapt, as a game mechanic, would allow the player to choose from three buffs (selected from a pool of ten) to be applied to your minion(s).

This mechanic was used exclusively in the Un’Goro expansion and represented 14 out of the 51 Random cards within Un’Goro. If we subtract the 14 Adapt-based cards, we are much closer, but still higher than earlier expansions at 37 random-based cards. We do see an increase in the percentage of RNG-based cards per expansion from 2014 to 2016. Only in 2017 do we begin to see an apparent decrease in the percentage of RNG-based cards compared to expansions earlier in the year. Another factor to examine came in the form of Hearthstone's first expansion rotation. This rotation occurred at the same time when Un’Goro was released.

This caused a large number of cards to be retired, moving them out of the “Standard” rotation and into the “Wild” format. The difference between Wild and Standard is the cards permitted to be played. Standard holds only the newest expansions while Wild includes all cards ever made. The removal of cards from Standard to Wild represents Blizzard's desire to replace or supplement some of the RNG based cards that had been removed with this rotation.

Are Battlecrys more common with RNG or Deathrattles?



Objective:

I wanted to give users a more interactive way to see the cards. After all, not everyone plays Hearthstone.
I embedded both RNG-based Battlecries and Deathrattles into my site to give the user some freedom to explore. However, these graphs lack discrete values, but this way users can see the cards and get a better feel for the game while still answering the question: which of the two has more interaction with RNG? It seems that Deathrattles have more cards overall than Battlecries. Battlecries give you the benefit right away, however, Deathrattles cards must die before the reward can be used. So, what we see is far more Deathrattles with random effects than Battlecries.

It occurred to me while I was trying to find out which of the two was more common, I also needed to define what common meant in the context of Hearthstone. As to why that is, I can only speculate. But, my intuition tells me: knowing that Battlecries are more powerful than Deathrattle in most cases, it is likely that Blizzard wanted to create more powerful cards in general, so they paired RNG components with more Battlecries.
